# Fitwell — Brand & Commercial Guardrails

Load this before writing or proposing anything customer-facing: copy, offers, pricing,
campaigns, emails, ads, landing pages. These are the non-negotiables. The depth lives in
`specs/strategy/` (see the map at the bottom) — this file is the guardrail layer that
keeps proposals on-brand. When a specific task needs detail, read the linked doc.

## Who we are

Fitwell Buckle Co. makes **precision micro-adjust watch buckles and links** — the only real
micro-adjust solution for watch *straps* (not just bracelets/deployants). Premium,
engineered, founder-led. ~91% gross margin, but **margin is not the lever — positioning is.**
We sell the fix to a real, physical problem, at full price, to people who care about getting
it right.

## Commercial guardrails (the non-negotiables)

1. **Never discount to lower the entry. Never lead with price.** Cutting price to convert a
   hesitant buyer tells the market the price was never real and caps us at the cheap-OEM-buckle
   anchor we work to escape. The value story carries the sale, not a coupon. If a buyer hesitates,
   the answer is more conviction (fit, finish, founder, guarantee, social proof) — **not less money.**
2. **On-brand offers reward buying *more*, not paying *less*.** Outfitting / volume incentives
   (e.g. *20% off 3+*) lift AOV and speak to collectors finishing their straps — that's on-brand.
   Single-unit discounts, threshold-lowering, "cheaper way in" — off-brand. These are categorically
   different; do not collapse them.
3. **Set the comparison frame; never let them anchor to a $5 OEM tang buckle.** Anchor against the
   *wasted watch* (P5), the *deployant* it beats at $80–200+ (P2), or the *premium strap* it finishes
   (P3). The moment the mental anchor is a cheap buckle, the sale is lost regardless of quality.
4. **Protect premium + "only solution" credibility in every artifact.** No bargain language, no
   manufactured scarcity beyond real deadlines, no claims we can't stand behind (see #5).
5. **Tell the truth about the product.** No overclaiming the mechanism. (E.g. the link loosens
   on-wrist easily but is hard to *tighten* on-wrist — say "set once," don't promise effortless
   on-the-fly tightening.) Honest copy is part of the premium.

## Positioning (how we frame the product)

- **Lead with the outcome — the *between-holes / make-it-actually-fit* fix.** It's the retained
  value (~half the review corpus credits it): "the fit that falls between two holes," "finally
  fits," "the watch you stopped wearing, wearable again." This anchors PDPs, product story, email.
- **On-wrist micro-adjust motion is the *acquisition hook*, not the retained value.** It's the
  scroll-stopper in paid creative. **Keep it in ads; do NOT strip it on review evidence** — reviews
  are blind to what drove the click. But don't lead PDP/product copy with it. (Full logic: H14.)
- **Surface the defensible secondary benefits** — finish/aesthetics and founder/service. Both are
  strong in reviews and harder for a big-brand copycat to clone than the mechanism. Current copy
  under-uses them.
- **M1 vs M4:** M1 *replaces* the buckle (swappable pin/tang straps); M4 *adds a link* between strap
  and existing closure (deployants, buckles you can't/won't swap, straps out of holes). Complementary,
  the natural cross-sell pair — not either/or.

## Audience discipline

- **Every artifact declares a persona (P1–P5 / B1–B6) and a funnel stage.** If you can't name the
  cell, read `specs/strategy/` before building. No untagged events, no unanchored pages.
- **Right message to the right list.** Don't send owners' copy to prospects. An "outfit the rest of
  your collection / buy 3+" message assumes prior ownership — it has no entry point for a never-buyer.
  Prospects convert on *belief at full price* (problem, proof, fit, guarantee, size finder), not on
  a discount and not on collection-completion framing.
- **Premium buyers (P1 Outfitters/Collectors) don't compare on price** — don't lead them with it.

## Voice

- Speak in the customer's own language (from the review corpus): *"between holes," "between sizes,"
  "tweener," "fit just so," "make the watch I stopped wearing wearable again," "as comfortable as my
  bracelet."* See `vocabulary-map.md`.
- Confident, precise, founder-honest. Problem → fix → proof. Not salesy, not bargain-bin, not hype.
- Cadence is set for the slowest sustainable week, not a quota — quality over volume.
