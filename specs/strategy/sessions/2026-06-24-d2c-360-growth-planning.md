## 2. The plan (the 360, brought to life)

Two engines running concurrently from week 1:

- **Growth engine (DTC):** Meta + TikTok to get discovered → Google + (committed)
  YouTube to close → TikTok Shop for direct, creator-driven sales → email/SMS to retain.
- **Survival engine:** the wholesale/OEM ramp + cost discipline carry the burn
  while DTC scales.

Workstreams:

- **Content engine (the bottleneck, fixed first):** founder-fronted blitz (3 shoot
  sessions, ~6 hrs) + AI pipeline (one shoot → many vertical + 16:9 cuts) + creator
  UGC. Real footage required for the on-wrist demo (AI can't render reflective metal
  / hands). Organic floor 3 IG + 3 TikTok + 1 YT Short/wk; organic winners → paid in 7–14 days.
- **Creators:** 735 scored creators (watch_score / fit_score / cross-platform).
  Reality of the motion: **most small creators just get a free buckle and post;
  a few bigger ones get paid a little; many aren't paid; only *some* get a discount
  code.** So attribution is partial by design. The load-bearing loop: creator UGC →
  Meta retargeting within 7–14 days (without it retargeting fatigues by ~day 30).
- **Paid:** Meta awareness (volume lever) + retargeting + consideration; Google
  brand/PMax; committed YouTube. Content-first — big paid dollars hold until ~August.
- **Pages & CRO:** fix the product-page → add-to-cart leak (~5% vs ~10% target) via
  Microsoft Clarity + customer interviews + ship obvious fixes; verify conversion
  tracking (Meta CAPI / Google) before spend. Shopify audit ~$4k one-time in July.
- **Email & text (Klaviyo):** lead with **campaigns** (the real gap), not just
  flows. Welcome flow live (+27.6% LTV). Build post-purchase (D1/D14/D21/D30),
  win-back, cross-sell; stand up SMS. Newsletter "The Micro-Adjust" exists.
- **TikTok Shop:** stand up as a parallel channel; fulfillment decision pending
  (Fulfilled-by-TikTok vs TikTok shipping labels — note: self-shipping ended Feb 2026).

The compounding *between* workstreams is the plan — if one lags (e.g. no creator
UGC) the others starve. Run them at once.

## 4. Financial model (conservative case)

**Unit economics (per order, from [[../../ops/domains/costs]] + Tom):**
AOV $62.14 · 1.6 items/order · COGS $3.65/unit · shipping paid $12.12 / collected
$4.65 · packaging $1 · fees ~2.9%+30¢ → **contribution ≈ $45.59/order (~73% of AOV)**.
Product gross margin is ~91%; shipping is the main drag (~$7.47/order). Margin is
*per order*, not per $40 buckle.

**Inputs / assumptions:**
- Starting cash **$375k** (post back-expenses; raise was $485k).
- Burn ~**$60k/mo total**, of which ~**$55k is non-marketing** (≈$32k founder
  salaries, started May 2026, + ~$23k ops/legal/dev/G&A); marketing rides on top.
- **DTC revenue ramp:** Jul $15k → Sep $40k → Dec $80k → Jun '27 $115k/mo
  (decelerating in H1 '27).
- **Wholesale/OEM:** start **$15k/mo, ramp 20%/mo** → ~$45k Jan, ~$111k Jun.
  Contribution margin ~65% (conservative blend). *This is the survival engine — the
  single biggest cash variable.*
- **Marketing spend** ramps ~$18k (Jul) → ~$58k (Jun), **~$504k/yr** (see §5).
- Model mechanics: contribution = DTC×0.73 + W/OEM×0.65; opex = $55k fixed + 5% of
  revenue + scaling FT hires ($6k May/Jun); EBITDA = contribution − marketing − opex.

**Outcome:** cash **trough ~$120k (Feb '27)** (≈$255k drawdown), **EBITDA-positive
March '27**, recovering to **~$171k by June '27**. Blended return on paid ~2.7–2.8x.

**Kill-switch (Tom's floor):** blended ROAS < 2, or no clear path to $3–5k/day
within 90 days → pull back / reallocate.

**Targets by month 3:** 30–40 orders/day · CVR 2%+ · 30-day repeat 30%+ ·
email list +300/mo · creator 10 outreach/wk, 5+ posts/wk · grow 90-day LTV off ~$76 base.

## 6. Corrections & learnings from this session

- Margin is **per order ($45.59)**, not "$45 profit on a $40 buckle."
- **Google/YouTube floor is $7k/mo** — do not model below it.
- **No investor is funding ads** — it's committed Fitwell spend.
- Klaviyo is active-profile-billed and genuinely scales; tools-line ramp is justified.
- Starting wholesale/OEM at $15k (vs $28k) materially lowers the trough — the
  W/OEM ramp rate is the make-or-break cash variable.
