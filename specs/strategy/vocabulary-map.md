# Vocabulary Map (Customer Language)

Last updated: 2026-05-26

> **Status: first systematic extraction.** Drawn from the 110+
> Judge.me reviews exported 2026-05-26. Captures what customers
> actually say in their own words — for use in ad creative,
> landing-page copy, email copy, product page copy, and SEO targeting.
> Companion to [[personas]]. Refresh quarterly or when the review
> corpus grows by 25%+.

## How to Use This Map

- These are real customer phrases. Lift verbatim or near-verbatim.
- Frequency is not the only signal. A phrase that appears once but
  perfectly captures a persona is more valuable than a common but
  generic phrase.
- Customer phrases beat marketer phrases. When in doubt, use theirs.
- Negative-review language reveals trust objections that your copy
  must preempt.
- Phrase counts below are from a `grep -i` pass over the review CSV;
  semantically related phrases are grouped.

## Top-Line Findings

1. **"Between holes / between sizes"** is the dominant pain phrase —
   7 reviews use some form of it; one uses *"tweener"*. This is the
   single most common customer-voiced articulation of the problem and
   should anchor performance-ad copy for P4/P5.
2. **"Solution" / "solved"** appears in 9 reviews. Customers frame the
   product as a *solution to a known problem* — not a novelty. Copy
   should match this framing.
3. **Bracelet-envy is real but small (3 reviews) and untapped.** The
   two strongest customer-voiced articulations of the positioning
   *"micro-adjust on bracelets, now on straps"* exist in the data.
4. **"Rolex" appears zero times.** Validates the exclusion thesis.
5. **Trust objection has a specific phrase signature** (4 reviews):
   *"moves back" / "doesn't stay" / "extends itself" / "loosest
   position"*. Hero-video copy should preempt these.
6. **Delugs is the dominant strap-partner channel by far** (5 review
   mentions; next closest brand is named once).
7. **OCD-About-Fit is rare verbatim but the concept is widespread.**
   "OCD" appears once, "just so" once, "particular" twice — but
   precision-personality vocabulary appears across most P1/P2 reviews.

**Distinctive feature:** Physical pain language about fit, sizing, or
wrist size variation.

| Phrase | Example | Strength |
|---|---|---|

## Trust-Objection Language (Negative Reviews)

The exact phrasing customers use when the autoloosening complaint
surfaces. Your copy must preempt these. 4 reviews exhibit this
pattern.

| Phrase | Source |
|---|---|
| *"automatically moves to the loosest position when you buckle the watch"* | Erik Lysne (2★) |
| *"the tension in the shorter position is too weak, so it just extends itself when the strap is on your wrist"* | One dissatisfied customer (1★) |
| *"every time I set the micro adjust position, it just moves back to the last position, so it kind of defeats the point"* | Timothy Andrews (1★) |
| *"doesn't stay in the smaller position"* | No way Jose (2★) |

**Counterpoint — a 5★ review that explicitly preempts the same
concern:** *"I was worried the buckle might stretch to the longer
position by itself while wearing a heavy watch but the construction is
very solid and it takes a positive effort to change the extension"* —
Giulio Carena. This sentence structure (concern → reality) is a copy
template worth lifting.

**Copy direction:** Demonstrate that tight locks in. Hero video
showing buckle holding under tension is the highest-leverage trust
asset. Frame as design feature, not defense against complaint.

## Notable Absences

- **Zero Rolex mentions.** Validates the persona exclusion.
- **Zero mentions of Hodinkee / WatchUSeek / Reddit specifically.**
  These are inferred channels, not customer-stated. May be where the
  buyers ARE, but they don't name those sources in reviews.
- **Zero mentions of edema / medical conditions.** P5 Comfort buyers
  describe the symptom (swelling, fluctuation) but not medical
  vocabulary. Copy should match this — don't medicalize.
- **Very few mentions of YouTube creators by name.** Inferred influence
  channels but not surfaced organically in reviews.

## Update Cadence

- Re-extract quarterly, or whenever the review count grows by 25%+
  from the last extraction.
- Track *new* phrases that emerge — they may indicate a new persona,
  trigger mode, or product issue.
- Negative-review phrases worth refreshing more often (monthly) —
  they reveal what current copy isn't addressing.
- When new geographies activate (e.g., post-Hive EU expansion), check
  whether non-English phrases surface new metaphors worth translating
  rather than literally translating English copy.
